Sobre Juno: New Origins e Vizzy

Juno: New Origins é um jogo de simulação espacial onde jogadores constroem e pilotam naves com física realista, explorando planetas e realizando missões em um sistema solar fictício.

Vizzy é a ferramenta de programação visual do Juno: New Origins, usada para automatizar naves com scripts visuais, controlando ações como navegação, estágios e eventos (ex.: colisões, órbitas).

Convenções de Notação

  • ...: Indica um espaço onde qualquer valor ou expressão pode ser inserido.
  • [...]: Indica que apenas opções pré-programadas no Vizzy podem ser usadas.
  • |: Indica que um bloco de código pode ser inserido na área.
  • || (opção do jogo): Indica uma funcionalidade específica do Vizzy, como criar variáveis ou expressões.

10. Subdivisões Detalhadas

Operators: [...] of [...]

  1. [ABS] of [...]: Returns the absolute value of the number.
  2. [Floor]: Returns largest integer smaller than or equal to the number.
  3. [Ceiling]: Returns smallest integer larger than or equal to the number.
  4. [Round]: Rounds the number to the closest integer.
  5. [Sqrt]: Calculates the square root of the number.
  6. [Sin]: Calculates the sine of the number.
  7. [Cos]: Calculates the cosine of the number.
  8. [Tan]: Calculates the tangent of the number.
  9. [Asin]: Calculates the arc-sine of the number, in radians.
  10. [Acos]: Calculates the arc-cosine of the number, in radians.
  11. [Atan]: Calculates the arc-tangent of the number, in radians.
  12. [Ln]: Calculates the natural logarithm of the number.
  13. [Log]: Calculates the logarithm base 10 of the number.
  14. [Deg2rad]: Converts the number from degrees to radians.
  15. [Rad2deg]: Converts the number from radians to degrees.

Friendly [...] [...]

  1. [Acceleration]: The acceleration in m/s² as text formatted in a user-friendly way.
  2. [Angular Velocity]: The angular velocity in degrees per second as text formatted in a user-friendly way.
  3. [Coordinate]: The latitude and longitude given a Lat, lon, AGL vector. AGL will be included if not 0.
  4. [Density]: The density in kg/m³ as text formatted in a user-friendly way.
  5. [Distance]: The distance in meters as text formatted in a user-friendly way.
  6. [Energy]: The energy in Joules as text formatted in a user-friendly way.
  7. [Force]: The force in Newtons as text formatted in a user-friendly way.
  8. [Specific Impulse]: The ISP in seconds as text formatted in a user-friendly way.
  9. [Mass]: The mass in kg as text formatted in a user-friendly way.
  10. [Power]: The power in Watts as text formatted in a user-friendly way.
  11. [Pressure]: The pressure in Pascals as text formatted in a user-friendly way.
  12. [Temperature]: The temperature in Kelvin as text formatted in a user-friendly way.
  13. [Time]: The time as text formatted in a user-friendly way showing a unique unit with decimal values.
  14. [Time (date)]: The time as text formatted in a user-friendly way, separating day, hours, minutes, and seconds.
  15. [Velocity]: The velocity in m/s as text formatted in a user-friendly way.

[...,...,...] [...]

  1. [X]: Returns the X component of the vector.
  2. [Y]: Returns the Y component of the vector.
  3. [Z]: Returns the Z component of the vector.
  4. [Length]: Returns the magnitude/length of the vector.
  5. [Norm]: Returns a vector in the same direction, but ensures its length is 1.

[...,...,...] [...] [...,...,...]

  1. [Angle]: Calculates the angle between two vectors in degrees.
  2. [Clamp]: Clamps the magnitude of the left vector to the magnitude of the right vector or constant number.
  3. [Cross]: Calculates the cross product of the two vectors.
  4. [Dot]: Calculates the dot product of the two vectors.
  5. [Dist]: Calculates the distance between the two vectors.
  6. [Min]: Returns a vector made of the minimum components of each vector.
  7. [Max]: Returns a vector made of the maximum components of each vector.
  8. [Project]: Projects the left vector onto the right vector.
  9. [Scale]: Multiplies two vectors component-wise.
